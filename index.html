About

Welcome! I am a full-time Research Assistant (RA) in the Economic Policy Studies department at the American Enterprise Institute for Public Policy Research (AEI). I primarily support the research of PhD economists and Senior Fellows Stan Veuger, Vincent Smith, and Paul Kupiec. In summer 2025, I will be moving to Stanford University and joining the Stanford Institute for Economic Policy Research (SIEPR) as a Predoctoral Research Fellow, working with Professors Ran Abramitzky and Rebecca Lester. My own research interests lie at the intersection of labor and urban economics, public finance, and the integration of causal inference and machine learning methods. I earned my BA in the Quantitative Economics Concentration from the Department of Economics at the University of Hawaii at Manoa in summer 2024. Before joining AEI, I worked as an RA at the University of Hawaii Economic Research Organization (UHERO), a Research Fellow at the Center for Entrepreneurship and Economic Education at Hawaii Pacific University, and a Research Associate with the Grassroot Institute of Hawaii. I plan to pursue a PhD in economics and/or public policy in the near future.
