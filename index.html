T. M. Jensen Ahokovi

I study how people, businesses, markets, and public policy interact to shape housing, work, and economic opportunity.

At the Stanford Institute for Economic Policy Research and the Stanford GSB TAX Lab.

Labor and urban economics

Studies how people choose and find work, how firms hire, and how housing, cities, and local economies shape those choices.

Public finance

Studies how taxes, government spending, and public programs affect people, businesses, and the wider economy.

Economic history

Studies how economies, institutions, and living standards changed over time, and what the past can teach us about economic questions today.

About

I am a predoctoral research fellow at SIEPR and the Stanford GSB TAX Lab, where I work with Ran Abramitzky and Rebecca Lester.

Previously, I was a research assistant in Economic Policy Studies at the American Enterprise Institute, primarily supporting Stan Veuger, Vincent Smith, and Paul Kupiec.

I earned a BA in Economics, cum laude, with a Quantitative Economics Concentration from the University of Hawai‘i at Mānoa in 2024.
